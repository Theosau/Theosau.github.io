I am a graduate student in Mechanical Engineering at UC Berkeley, where I work on Physics Informed Machine Learning (ML) for cardio-vascular and personalized applications, advised by Prof. Shawn Shadden. I have 5+ years of demonstrated experience in programming, building ML, deep learning, and symbolic AI models for various applications such as computer vision, physical modeling and behavioural understanding (in criminology). I love challenging projects which require building innovative and creative solutions, to positively impact society.

I wish to combine my experience in computer science and mechanical engeineering to build innovative products. I am currently working on personalized medicine application, and have led a group of 5 to develop a bionic hand for below-elbow amputees in the past.

Prior to joinging Berkeley, I recieved an BEng in Mechanical Engineering and an MSc in Computing (Specialism AI and ML), both from Imperial College London. During these, I had the chance of collaborating with researchers such as Salvador Navarro-Martinez, Alessandra Russo, and Dalal Alrajeh.

In the summer 2022, I worked in Ansys' San Jose CTO team on a new generation of PDE numerical solvers powered by physics informred ML. Previously, I gained industrial experience in different tech-focused start-ups: I applied ML to supply chain challenges at Vekia, and to quality control and anomaly detection on images at Scortex (which has been aqcuired by Trigo group).
